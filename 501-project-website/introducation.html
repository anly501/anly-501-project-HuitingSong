Project Introduction

Energy is an important material basis of the national economy, and the future fate of the country depends on the control of energy. The degree of development and effective use of energy and per capita consumption are important indicators of production and living standards. The energy resources that we come into contact with on a daily basis are natural gas, oil, electricity, etc. As countries' economies and populations grow, so does their energy consumption. The following graph of global energy consumption from One World in Data shows that, with the exception of the year 2020 when a new coronavirus causes a global stagnation and the economic crisis in 2008, the demand for different energy sources has been increasing every year since the 1990s.

With this growing demand for energy, people are facing environmental and climate issues. The environmental problems directly related to energy production and consumption include air pollution, climate change, water pollution, thermal pollution, and solid waste disposal. The emission of air pollutants from fossil fuel combustion is the major cause of urban air pollution. Therefore, more and more people have started to publicize the dangers of global warming and call for the protection of the environment. So with the awakening of everyone's awareness of protecting the environment now, are we really saving energy and reducing emissions?

Therefore, the main research object of my project is energy consumption. I will approach the study of energy consumption in two directions:

First, analyzing people's perceptions of energy consumption.

Since the only people can contribute to and change the environment in this Earth, human actions are important if we want to achieve the goal of environmental protection. Therefore, I would like to find out what people's perceptions of energy consumption are, so that I can determine how likely they are to reduce energy consumption.

Second, predicting the consumption of different types of energy in the next decade.

As the market for clean energy continues to expand and become more widely used, like electric cars and solar power, I suspect that people's consumption and demand for traditional energy will trend downward in the future compared to today. The reduction of traditional energy consumption can reduce the production of environmental pollutants to a certain extent, so I would like to analyze whether environmental problems can be reduced by predicting the consumption of different types of energy in the next ten years.

Here is the 10 questions to addressing my project

  1. 1. What does increased energy consumption mean for people?
  2. 2. Does clean energy matter for the environmental protection?
  3. 3. Who contributes the most to energy consumption, residence, industry, or commercial?
  4. 4. How do people think about global warming?
  5. 5. Which energy source will be consumed less in the next decade?
  6. 6. What will the overall energy consumption level look like in the next decade?
  7. 7. Will demand for traditional energy sources, such as oil, decline?
  8. 8. Will people choose new energy products? Such as electric cars?
  9. 9. Can new energy replace traditional energy?
  10. 10. Will new energy consumption continue to grow in the next decade?
