Project Conclusion

Energy and environmental issues have always been a concern around the world. The United States, as the world's leading energy consumer and advocate of ecological conservation, has always had a long-term plan. As the population base and industrial structure change, energy demand and consumption are continuously growing. These increases are driving the nation's economic and national income growth. However, behind these increases, people are also facing the consequences of environmental degradation. So, in today's background where everyone is advocating energy conservation and environmental protection, are we really making a change?

To this question, my answer is: "We are changing". From this study, people have their own ideas about environmental issues, at least people take to public platforms on Twitter to call for environmental protection and discuss energy issues. And from the point of view of people's electricity consumption habits, everyone is at a normal consumption level, and there is no serious unscientific way of consumption. People's electricity consumption habits will change with climate change, so some major energy consumption is inevitable. And, with the birth of new and clean energy sources, people are discussing energy conversion and considering the use of solar energy. These are things that we don't normally notice, but I felt them in this project. People are worried about environmental improvement, but they are also actively promoting and using new energy sources./font>

Answering the 10 Questions

1. What does increased energy consumption mean for people?

Traditionally, rising energy consumption can represent a variety of possibilities. For example, population growth, increased income or good economic conditions. For the general public, people may not be aware of what increasing energy consumption is all about. It's hard to perceive the meaning behind such a potentially abstract term when you're in the big picture. From the data I collected from Twitter users, we can see that people talk about energy consumption, but it is easy to see from the keyword associations that people discuss that the word energy consumption is still abstract and difficult for people to perceive. However, when I see from another data group that people are concerned about the environment and new energy sources, I think it shows that people are actually concerned about energy issues, but not energy consumption issues. They are more concerned about energy transformation and protecting the environment.

2. Does clean energy matter for the environmental protection?

In my second Twitter user data crawl, I used keywords like "oil", "gas", "solar power", "wind power", "environment" to scratch users' attitudes about these four kinds of energy. Oil and gas are energy in the traditional sense, while solar and wind are what we now call clean energy. In calculating and mapping the frequency of words in their informational texts, I found that solar power was discussed most often. We can also find other words in the cloud, such as 'lead coal' and 'expensive angry', which also indicate people's emotions and attitudes towards different energy sources. The emergence of these attitudes may also indicate a change in the way people view the types of energy sources nowadays. From once relying on traditional energy sources to now supporting clean energy sources, the awareness of environmental protection is growing. For the future, clean energy will occupy a larger and larger share of the market. The emergence of various new energy products, such as electric cars, and the widespread promotion of people from all walks of life, the use of new energy will only increase, so we can achieve the purpose of environmental protection by reducing harmful emissions from traditional energy sources.
